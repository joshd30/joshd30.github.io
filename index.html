STEP 3: Exploratory data analysis

The exploratory analysis is a crucial step in the data analysis process where we seek to understand the patterns, trends, and relationships in the data. During this step, we use various tools and techniques such as visualization, statistical tests, and data summaries to gain insights into the data and identify any potential issues or anomalies. The goal of exploratory analysis is to uncover hidden patterns and trends, formulate hypotheses, and identify areas of the data that require further investigation or analysis.

In the exploratory analysis stage, we typically start by getting a high-level overview of the data, such as its size, structure, and overall distribution. This can be done by looking at the data's summary statistics, such as the mean, median, and standard deviation, and by creating visualizations such as histograms, scatter plots, and box plots. These tools help us to understand the distribution and dispersion of the data, identify any outliers or anomalies, and get a sense of the overall shape of the data.

We can also use statistical tests and techniques such as t-tests, ANOVA, and regression analysis to explore relationships between variables and test hypotheses about the data. For example, we might use a t-test to compare the mean of two groups, or use regression analysis to understand the relationship between two variables and make predictions about one variable based on the other.

Overall, the exploratory analysis stage is an iterative process where we constantly refine our understanding of the data and formulate new hypotheses as we uncover new patterns and trends. It is an essential step in the data analysis process that helps us to understand the data and identify the most important factors and variables to focus on in our analysis.

def top_streamers(language):
    
    filter = language.title()
    streamers_language = df.loc[df["Language"] == filter]
    streamers_language.reset_index(drop=True, inplace=True)
    
    channels = streamers_language["Channel"]
    total_watch_time = streamers_language["Watch time(Minutes)"]
    
    fig = plt.figure(figsize=(10, 6))
    plot = sns.barplot(x=channels[:10], y=total_watch_time[:10], palette="muted")
    plt.title("Top 10 most viewed channels in {language} in 2020")
    plt.xticks(rotation=45)
    plt.show()

STEP 4: Hypothesis testing

My hypothesis is: Streamers who have higher average viewer counts and more followers will be more successful on Twitch, as indicated by their watch time and streaming time.

To test this, I will do a linear regression test which will confirm or reject my hypothesis. I got the desired features and then split the data as well as t raining it. After importing r2_score and mean_squared_error. I got the data necessary to show a proper correlation. The figure below represents the results.

In [15]:
features = df[['Watch time(Minutes)','Stream time(minutes)','Peak viewers','Average viewers','Followers','Views gained']]
target = df['Followers gained']
X_train,X_test,y_train,y_test = train_test_split(features,target,test_size = 0.2,random_state = 101)
scaler = StandardScaler()
scaler.fit(X_train)
X_train = scaler.transform(X_train)
X_test = scaler.transform(X_test)
model  = LinearRegression()
model.fit(X_train,y_train)
predictions = model.predict(X_test)
from sklearn.metrics import r2_score, mean_squared_error
print('r2 score:'+str(r2_score(y_test, predictions)))
print('RMSE :'+str(np.sqrt(mean_squared_error(y_test, predictions))))

STEP 5: Conclusion

In conclusion, the analysis of the Twitch streamer dataset has shed light on the various factors that contribute to a streamer's success on the platform. Through the use of visualization, statistical tests, and machine learning techniques, we were able to uncover hidden patterns and trends in the data and gain a deeper understanding of the relationships between different variables.

One of the most striking findings from the analysis was the strong correlation between streaming time and the number of followers gained. This suggests that streamers who put in more time on the platform are more likely to see a corresponding increase in their follower count.
